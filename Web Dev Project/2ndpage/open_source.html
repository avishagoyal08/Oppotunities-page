OPEN SOURCE

1) Google Summer of Code

Google Summer of Code (GSoC) is a program started by Google to promote open-source software development among university students. It has completed 16 years since its inception.

Selection Process: The program involves mentors from a few organizations. First, the organizations apply to Google. Once the organizations are selected, the students can apply to the organizations.

Stipend: 1500 USD

2)MLH Fellowship

Major League Hacking (MLH), founded in 2013, is a global community for student developers. MLH recently launched a program known as MLH fellowship. In this 12-week remote program, students collaborate on Open Source projects with general requirements that align with their interests. Students can participate from their homes. It has three main tracks – Explorer, Open Source, and Externship. One can gain the skills required to be a successful engineer, like writing good documentation, open-source best practices, communication, etc.

Selection Process:

  • Application Review: Going through your resume, essay questions, etc
  • Code Review: Looking at the code sample to ensure it's of a high enough quality
  • Initial Interview: Chance to get to know you and find out why you want to be part of the Fellowship
  • Technical Interview: Chance to show your technical skills by going through your code sample
  • Final Review: The best applications are selected and sent the acceptance letter
Timeline:
  • Spring Batch: Jan – Apr
  • Summer Batch. May – Aug.
  • Fall Batch. Sept-Dec.
Stipend: need-based stipend of up to $5000

3)Google Season of Docs

Something that open-source projects don't get enough of - good documentation. Enter Google Season of Docs. GSoD is a new program by Google where technical writers and open source organizations work on open source projects. The technical writers write documentation for open source organizations and get paid in return by Google. It is a worthwhile platform for students to learn from industry experts and to work on open-source projects.

Eligibility: Age more than 18 years, should have previous experience as a technical writer, and should be eligible to work in your country of residence.

Stipend: 2400 - 6600 USD

4)Outreachy

Outreachy is an excellent program for students. It aims to support diversity in open source. It is a 3-month remote internship, with only a handful selected across the world. The Outreachy internship projects focus on programming, documentation, project marketing, etc.

Eligibility: Age more than 18 years, available for internship from December - March

Selection Process: First, fill out the initial application. Next, wait a few days to have it approved. During this time, Outreachy organizers may reach out to confirm applicants’ time commitments or ask them to clarify their essays. Make sure you check your email daily for updates. After the application is approved, the candidate can choose the projects of their liking.

Stipend: 5500 USD
